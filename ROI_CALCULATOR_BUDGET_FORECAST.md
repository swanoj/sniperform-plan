## PART 6: LTV:CAC ANALYSIS

### How Much Can You Spend Per Customer?

**Formula:**
```
Max CPA = (Customer LTV × Target LTV:CAC ratio) / Safety margin
```

**Example:**
- Customer LTV = $150 (repeat purchases + referrals)
- Target LTV:CAC = 3:1 (industry healthy)
- Safety margin = 20% (buffer for variances)
- Max CPA = ($150 × 3) / 1.2 = $375 per customer

**But more conservatively:**
- If LTV = $150 and you want 2:1 ratio (safer)
- Max CPA = ($150 × 2) = $300 per customer

**How to calculate your actual CPA:**
```
CPA = Ad spend / Number of customers acquired

Example:
Month 1 spend: $7,800
Customers acquired: ~100 (assuming 0.75% CVR on 13,000 visitors)
CPA = $7,800 / 100 = $78

Is $78 sustainable?
If LTV = $150:
LTV:CAC ratio = $150 / $78 = 1.9:1

That's healthy (1.9:1 is acceptable, 3:1 is great).
```

## PART 7: BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS

### At What ROAS Do You Break Even?

**Formula:**
```
Break-even ROAS = 1 / Gross margin %

Example:
Gross margin = 65%
Break-even ROAS = 1 / 0.65 = 1.54x
```

**Interpretation:** You need ROAS of **1.54x** to cover ad spend + COGS and break even on ads alone.

**But wait:** That doesn't include operational costs (Madigix, Shopify, email platform, etc.)

**More realistic break-even:**
- Ad spend: $15,000
- Operational costs: ~$1,500 (Madigix + tools)
- Total costs: $16,500
- Revenue needed: $16,500
- Gross profit margin: 65%
- Required ROAS: $16,500 / (0.65 × average order) = variable
