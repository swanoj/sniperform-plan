# Sniperform Video Production Guide
## 5 Scripts for Thursday Shoot

**Shoot Date:** Thursday [Date]  
**Formats Needed:** Vertical video (9:16), Reels, Shorts, TikToks  
**Total Production Time:** 4–5 hours  
**Total Scripts:** 5 (60s, 45s, 30s, 45s, 60s)

---

## PRODUCTION SPECS

### Technical Requirements
- **Camera:** Any smartphone (iPhone 14+, Pixel 6+) or mirrorless
- **Audio:** Lavalier mic or built-in (backup with external USB mic)
- **Lighting:** Natural gym light + ring light (if available)
- **Background:** Dark gym environment (high contrast preferred)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 9:16 vertical (mobile-first)
- **Frame Rate:** 24fps or 30fps

### Equipment Checklist
- [ ] Camera (phone or mirrorless)
- [ ] External microphone (lav mic preferred)
- [ ] Ring light or portable LED panels
- [ ] Tripod with phone mount
- [ ] Sniperform grips (product shots)
- [ ] Barbell/dumbbells (for demonstration)
- [ ] Spirit level vial (prominent in shots)

### Location
- **Primary:** Well-lit gym with dark walls
- **Secondary:** Dark background, good contrast
- **Avoid:** Bright windows (causes backlight issues), cluttered backgrounds

## VIDEO #1: "Thicker Grips = Bigger Arms" (Control Hook)
**Length:** 60 seconds  
**Purpose:** Hero/control creative for paid ads  
**Formats:** Instagram Reel, TikTok, YouTube Short  
**Target Audience:** All ages (25–64)

### Script

```
[0–3 SECONDS: HOOK + TEXT OVERLAY]
On screen: "Thicker Grips = Bigger Arms"
Voiceover (energetic): "Your grip is limiting your gains."
Visual: Close-up of person's hands gripping a thin barbell, looking strained

[3–10 SECONDS: PROBLEM]
On screen: "Thin bar = CNS limits force output"
Voiceover: "Standard bars are thin. Your CNS sees this as unsafe."
Visual: Slow-motion of person struggling to hold weight, hand slipping slightly
Cut to: Anatomical diagram or text showing "Neural Inhibition"

[10–25 SECONDS: SOLUTION]
On screen: "Thicker grip = Neural drive unlocked"
Voiceover: "A thicker grip bypasses this. Your muscles can work harder."
Visual: Person picks up Sniperform grips, puts them on barbell
Close-up: Spirit level vial showing BALANCED position
Cut to: Person lifts with confidence, perfect form

[25–40 SECONDS: PROOF]
On screen: "More muscle activation = Bigger gains"
Voiceover: "Your biceps and triceps work 20–30% harder."
Visual: Split-screen comparison:
  - Left side: Thin bar (struggling)
  - Right side: Thick grip (powerful, controlled, muscle pump visible)
Cut to: Close-up of muscle activation, slow-motion of successful lift

[40–55 SECONDS: GUARANTEE]
On screen: "90-day guarantee. Risk free."
Voiceover: "Try it risk-free. 90-day guarantee."
Visual: Sniperform grips rotating on barbell, spirit level vial prominent
Text: "Trusted by 5,000+ serious lifters"

[55–60 SECONDS: CTA]
On screen: "Learn how" + link
Voiceover: "See why serious lifters switched."
Visual: Product hero shot (grips on bar), logo, CTA button
End frame: "sniperform.com"
```

### Production Notes
- **Tone:** Educational + empowering (not hype)
- **Pacing:** Slow at start (hook), accelerate through proof
- **Audio:** 
  - Voiceover: Natural, conversational (not robotic)
  - Background music: Energetic, gym-vibe (lofi hip-hop or electronic)
- **Color grade:** Dark gym aesthetic, teal accent lighting if possible
- **Key shots needed:**
  - 1. Struggling with thin bar (hands, close-up)
  - 2. Spirit level vial in action (close-up, well-lit)
  - 3. Successful lift with Sniperform (full body, muscle engagement visible)
  - 4. Product beauty shots (grips on bar, multiple angles)

## VIDEO #2: "Your Left Arm is Weaker" (Bilateral Balance Angle)
**Length:** 45 seconds  
**Purpose:** Awareness + differentiation (Sniperform exclusive)  
**Formats:** Instagram Reel, TikTok, YouTube Short  
**Target Audience:** Ages 25–44 (strength-focused)

### Script

```
[0–3 SECONDS: HOOK + STOP-THE-SCROLL]
On screen: "Your left arm is weaker. You just don't know it."
Voiceover (intriguing): "Most lifters are imbalanced."
Visual: Split-screen of person doing dumbbell curls — one side visibly working harder
Text overlay: "50–80% of lifters have 5–15% strength imbalance"

[3–12 SECONDS: PROBLEM REVEALED]
On screen: "This imbalance costs you gains"
Voiceover: "One side compensates. Energy leaks. Asymmetrical growth."
Visual: Close-up of barbell on shoulders — one side visibly lower than the other
Cut to: Anatomical animation or split-screen showing compensation pattern

[12–25 SECONDS: THE SOLUTION]
On screen: "Real-time feedback changes everything"
Voiceover: "What if you could SEE your imbalance?"
Visual: Person puts on Sniperform grips
Close-up: Spirit level vial showing LEVEL → person adjusts form → vial balances
Text overlay: "Spirit level vial = instant feedback"

[25–40 SECONDS: PROOF]
On screen: "One month: balanced strength gains"
Voiceover: "Fix the imbalance. Your strength becomes symmetrical."
Visual: Before/after split-screen:
  - Before: Uneven pull, off-balance
  - After: Perfect symmetry, powerful, controlled
Cut to: Person doing heavy row with perfect bilateral balance
Close-up of vial showing perfect level

[40–45 SECONDS: CTA]
On screen: "See the truth. Fix the imbalance." + link
Voiceover: "This is what serious lifters know."
Visual: Product hero shot
End frame: Logo + CTA
```

### Production Notes
- **Tone:** Revelatory, slightly urgent (you've been doing it wrong)
- **Key differentiator:** Spirit level vial must be VISIBLE and PROMINENT in every relevant shot
- **Critical shots needed:**
  - 1. Split-screen: uneven strength demonstration (clear visual imbalance)
  - 2. Spirit level vial in extreme close-up (show level indicator moving, then balanced)
  - 3. Before/after footage (same exercise, clearly worse form vs. balanced form)
  - 4. Product close-ups of the vial (hero shot of the patent)
- **Color grade:** High contrast, teal highlights on vial
- **Audio:** Mysterious intro, building energy through solution

## VIDEO #5: "The Bilateral Balance Problem Nobody Talks About" (Educational Explainer)
**Length:** 60 seconds  
**Purpose:** Deep educational content for organic reach + email  
**Formats:** YouTube Short, Instagram Reel, Blog embed  
**Target Audience:** All (education-first angle)

### Script

```
[0–5 SECONDS: HOOK + ANIMATION]
On screen: "Your imbalance is costing you 10+ pounds per arm"
Voiceover: "Here's something most lifters don't know."
Visual: Simple animation or infographic showing bilateral asymmetry concept
Text: "The bilateral balance problem"

[5–15 SECONDS: THE SCIENCE]
On screen: "Most people favor one side by 5–15%"
Voiceover: "Whether you're left or right dominant, your body has a preference."
Visual: Animation showing neurological bias (one hemisphere more developed)
Cut to: Real footage of person exercising, clearly favoring one side
Text: "This is normal. But it's costing you gains."

[15–30 SECONDS: THE CONSEQUENCE]
On screen: "What happens over time:"
Voiceover: "One side gets stronger. The other plays catch-up."
Sequence animation/text:
  - Week 1: "Slight imbalance emerges"
  - Week 4: "One side pulls harder"
  - Week 8: "Compensation patterns develop"
  - Week 12: "Asymmetrical muscle growth"
  - Month 6: "One arm visibly larger"
Visual: Split-screen comparison of uneven arm development
Text: "Plus: Injury risk, wasted energy, slow progress"

[30–45 SECONDS: WHY IT HAPPENS]
On screen: "Without feedback, you can't feel the imbalance"
Voiceover: "Your nervous system adapts to imbalance. It becomes normal."
Voiceover continues: "Your brain compensates so well, you never notice."
Visual: Brain animation or person training "blind" (eyes closed, can't see bar path)
Text: "The problem: No real-time feedback"

[45–55 SECONDS: THE FIX]
On screen: "What if you could SEE it?"
Voiceover: "Real-time balance feedback changes everything."
Visual: Spirit level vial in action during exercise
Close-up: Vial tilts, person feels it, adjusts form, vial levels
Voiceover: "Instantly you know. Instantly you correct."
Text: "Balanced training = symmetrical gains"

[55–60 SECONDS: CTA]
On screen: "Discover bilateral training"
Voiceover: "Learn more about balanced strength."
Visual: Product hero, logo, link
End frame: "sniperform.com"
```

### Production Notes
- **Tone:** Educational, slightly eye-opening ("nobody talks about...")
- **Visual style:** Mix of real footage + simple animations/infographics
- **Key requirement:** Animation/graphics showing the imbalance concept (critical for understanding)
- **Critical shots:**
  - 1. Real footage of uneven training (clear visual imbalance)
  - 2. Spirit level vial demonstrating the feedback (multiple angles)
  - 3. Before/after (optional but powerful)
  - 4. Simple animation/infographic explaining the science
- **Audio:** Calm, explanatory voiceover, educational background music
- **Editing:** Thoughtful pacing (not rushed), text overlays for emphasis

## PRE-SHOOT CHECKLIST

### Location Scouting (Do before Thursday)
- [ ] Confirm gym access + lighting
- [ ] Check wall colors (dark background preferred)
- [ ] Test audio (echo issues?)
- [ ] Plan camera angles (where will tripod go?)
- [ ] Confirm equipment access (barbell, dumbbells, etc.)

### Equipment Prep
- [ ] Charge all cameras/phones
- [ ] Test microphone (audio levels)
- [ ] Test lighting setup
- [ ] Test tripod stability
- [ ] Have backup battery packs

### Talent/Cast
- [ ] Confirm talent availability (if using someone else)
- [ ] Brief them on scripts (send beforehand)
- [ ] Plan wardrobe (gym clothes, fitted so movement is visible)
- [ ] Hair/makeup (minimal, natural gym look)

### Props
- [ ] Sniperform grips (multiple, in case damage)
- [ ] Barbell + weight plates
- [ ] Dumbbells (various sizes)
- [ ] Spirit level vial (ensure it's visible + functional)
- [ ] Backup products

### Post-Production
- [ ] Have Adobe Creative Suite or CapCut ready
- [ ] Download raw footage same day
- [ ] Start editing immediately (batch while fresh)
- [ ] Plan music/voiceover sources

## VOICEOVER NOTES

**Style:** Natural, conversational, not robotic  
**Pace:** Moderate (not rushed)  
**Tone varies by script:**
- Script 1 (Hero): Energetic, empowering
- Script 2 (Bilateral): Intriguing, revelatory
- Script 3 (5 Exercises): Quick, motivational
- Script 4 (Coach): Credible, expert, calm
- Script 5 (Explainer): Educational, thoughtful

**Recording options:**
- In-house (phone voice memo + USB mic)
- Professional (Fiverr, Rev.com, Synthesia for AI voiceover)
- Coach himself (if doing Script 4 as testimonial)

---

## POST-PRODUCTION EDITING TIPS

1. **Color Grading:** Dark gym aesthetic + teal accents on product/vial
2. **Text Overlays:** Use consistent font (match brand), clear and large
3. **Cuts:** Align with music beat for Scripts 1, 3, 4
4. **B-Roll:** Lots of close-ups of vial + muscle engagement
5. **Transitions:** Keep simple (cuts work better than fades for TikTok/Reels)
6. **Sound Design:** Gym sound bed + music + voiceover + impact sounds
